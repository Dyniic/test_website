About

A brief summary of my work history.

  • 1986-2002

    My Humble Beginnings

    The first 15 or 16 years of my career were spent honing my skills and expertise in electronics, mechanics, and robotics. I served in a variety of roles in 2 large businesses supporting manufacturing and new product design.

  • 2002-2009

    A Computer Tech is Born

    Ventured out to start my own computer repair business. The company mainly served home and small business users. My specialty was malware removal and system repairs. The timeframe from around 2004-2009 was where computer malware infections were rampant. Windows XP, while a great operating system, was extremely vulnerable to attack. The malware authors had also become really good at social engineering as a way to get their wares on the system. While malware is still a problem today it is nowhere near what it was during these years.

    I also supported hardware and software repairs/upgrades, network support, peripherals, backups, and other tech support functions as needed.

  • 2010 to Present

    IT Contractor

    Expenses were too high and trying to compete with the big boys became too difficult to keep the computer repair business doors open. I ventured out as an independent contractor and have worked with a variety of companies from small to large doing all phases of IT support since then, and continue that today.

  • July 2014

    A Web Developer in the Making

    In the summer of 2014 things were a little slow in the IT support world for me so I decided to go into a deep dive web developer learning journey. I've done some very basic coding over the years and even took some college courses in C/C++ and HTML/CSS.
    I started out reading a couple of books, taking some free online courses, and putting together some very basic web sites using tutorials. There is much to learn - HTML, CSS, Javascript, JQuery, PHP, MySQL, Frameworks, CMS such as Wordpress/Joomla, tooling/processes, and more. I continue to push forward every day learning something new and expanding my web development skills.
